Date to choose - Molina de Aragón & Barranco de la Hoz.

To commemorate two years of trekking excursions in the region of Guadalajara, the present creator of this website is organizing an excursion in the eastern area of ​​Guadalajara.

There is a vans rental service for those who do not want to bring their own vehicle.
It is recommended to wear comfortable shoes, in case the sun hits, and provisions. Important water, 2 liters per person.
Also something to cover the head as the sun is very dangerous and sun protection to avoid burning.

The route in principle is quite simple and will be more a walk, if we make the slope following the river we will avoid the sun but will have to go up to some viewpoint.

    

Visit to Molina de Aragón

    

We will arrive through the national road N-211 from Madrid or Guadalajara, with a route of 200km for Madrid and 120km for Guadalajara, with an estimated time from Madrid of 2 hours.     The departure will be at 9 am in Madrid and 10 in Guadalajara approximately arriving in Molina at around 11 am.     We will visit the castle and the helmet of the city and surroundings and at around 12:30 we will depart towards the Santurario de la Virgen de la Hoz.


         

Visit to the Ravine of the Sickle and River Gallo

    

Once we have visited the temple and the viewpoints we will make a march down the Gallo river and eat around.
 The views in these places are incredible, only comparable to other sites in Teruel and north of Cuenca.
